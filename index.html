Mahendra Singh Dhoni

Indian Cricketer

An Indian international cricketer who captained the Indian national team in limited-overs formats from 2007 to 2016

A TIMELINE

  • Dec 02, 2005 - Debut against Sri Lanka in Chennai. Makes 30 off 54 balls
  • Aug 07, 2007 – Dhoni is named as India captain for the 2007 ICC World T20 after senior stars pull out of the competition.
  • Sep 13, 2007 – Dhoni’s first match as Indian captain against Scotland is washed out.
  • Sep 14, 2007 – India beat Pakistan via a bowl-out in Dhoni’s first completed match as skipper.
  • Sep 19, 2007 – Dhoni is named captain of the Indian ODI team after Rahul Dravid resigns.
  • Sep 24, 2007 – Dhoni leads India to World T20 glory after beating Pakistan by five runs in the final.
  • Mar 04, 2008 – Under the captaincy of Dhoni, India win a historic ODI series in Australia for the first time ever.
  • Apr 11, 2008 – Dhoni leads India in Test cricket for the first time as they beat South Africa by eight wickets at Kanpur.
  • Nov 02, 2008 – Dhoni is made permanent skipper of the Indian Test team after Anil Kumble announces his retirement.
  • Apr 09, 2009 – Dhoni leads India to their first Test series victory in New Zealand in 41 years.
  • Dec 06, 2009 – India reach the pinnacle of Test cricket as Dhoni guides his men to the number one spot on the ICC Test Rankings.
  • Jun 24, 2010 – India win the Asia Cup for the first time in 15 years after beating Sri Lanka by 81 runs in the final.
  • Apr 02, 2011 – Dhoni is named Man of the Match in the final as India win the World Cup on home soil.
  • Aug 22, 2011 – Dhoni suffers his first series loss as Test captain as England humiliate India 4-0 in a four-match Test series.
  • Oct 29, 2016 – Dhoni’s last match as India captain (not known at the time) is their series win over New Zealand.
  • Jan 4, 2017 – Dhoni resigns as captain of the ODI and T20I teams, but will continue to play for India in the two formats.
